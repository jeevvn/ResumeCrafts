Contact

Let's start with the basics. To ensure employers can reach you, input at least your name, email, and phone number.

Adding a professional photo can help employers remember you

Personal Information

Contact Details

Additional Information (Optional)

Contact Tips

  • Use a professional email address. One that includes your full name helps.
  • Gmail is the preferred email client. Addresses from 'Yahoo' or 'Hotmail' can seem outdated.
  • Include a phone number with a professional voicemail message.
  • Make sure your LinkedIn profile is up-to-date and matches your resume.
  • Only include social media links if they are professional and relevant.

Education

List your educational qualifications starting with the most recent degree or certification.

Education #1

Education Tips

  • List your most recent degree first.
  • Include your GPA if it's 3.0 or higher.
  • Mention relevant coursework if you have limited work experience.
  • Include study abroad programs if applicable.
  • Add your expected graduation date if you're still in school.

Work Experience

Detail your professional experience. Focus on achievements and responsibilities relevant to your target job.

Experience #1

Experience Tips

  • Start each bullet point with a strong action verb.
  • Quantify achievements when possible (e.g. "Increased sales by 20%").
  • Tailor your experience to match the job you're applying for.
  • List your most recent position first.
  • Keep descriptions concise but impactful.

Certifications

List any professional certifications that are relevant to your career.

Certification #1

Certification Tips

  • Include only certifications relevant to the job you're applying for
  • List the most recent certifications first
  • Include expiration dates if applicable
  • Provide credential IDs for verification
  • Mention if you're currently pursuing a certification

Skills Tips

  • Include both hard and soft skills
  • Tailor your skills to match the job description
  • List skills in order of proficiency or relevance
  • Be specific - instead of "Microsoft Office", list "Excel PivotTables"
  • Include industry-specific keywords for ATS scanning

Professional Summary

Write a compelling summary that highlights your experience and achievements.

Typically 3-5 sentences highlighting your key qualifications

Optional for entry-level candidates or career changers

Key Achievements

Summary Tips

  • Start with your job title and years of experience
  • Include 2-3 key achievements with quantifiable results
  • Mention your top skills and areas of expertise
  • Tailor it to the specific job you're applying for
  • Keep it concise - recruiters spend only seconds scanning resumes

References

Provide professional references who can vouch for your qualifications.

Note on References

Most resumes don't include references directly. Instead, you can note "References available upon request". However, it's good to have them prepared separately.

Reference #1

Reference Tips

  • Always ask permission before listing someone as a reference
  • Choose references who know your work well and will speak positively
  • Provide each reference with a copy of your resume
  • Include 3-5 professional references
  • Update your references periodically

Final Tips

  • Review all information for accuracy and spelling.
  • Consider having someone else proofread your resume.
  • Save your resume as a PDF unless otherwise requested.
  • Name your file professionally: "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf"
  • Update your resume every 6 months even if you're not job searching.
